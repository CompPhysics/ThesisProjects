Overall Impression

This is an unusually mature piece of work for a master's thesis. The
writing is confident, the physical intuition is genuine, and the
three-layer organizational framework (ansatz → training →
optimization) is genuinely clarifying. That said, there are real
weaknesses worth addressing, and the gap between the thesis's
ambitions and its execution is uneven across chapters.

Chapter 1: Quantum Theory

Strengths. The coverage is competent and correctly ordered. The Kato
cusp derivation in 2D is presented more carefully than in most
textbooks, and the structural diagnostics section (§1.7) is unusually
concrete — it anticipates the diagnostics chapter rather than leaving
them to emerge ad hoc.

Weaknesses.

The chapter tries to cover too much territory too thinly. Second
quantization occupies three pages and is then explicitly abandoned:
the author acknowledges it "does not appear directly in the
variational neural ansatz." This is honest, but it raises the question
of why it is here at all. At master's level, the reader is better
served by depth on what is actually used.  The FCI section (§1.5) is
similarly underweight for the role it plays. FCI is invoked throughout
Chapter 6 as a benchmark, but the thesis never clearly states which
basis was used for the FCI calculations, what truncation was applied,
or whether the FCI numbers are self-computed or drawn from Haas
(2024). This ambiguity propagates into Chapter 6 where the comparisons
are made.  The Wigner diagnostic section (§1.7) duplicates Chapter 5
substantially. The reader encounters the same formulas for g(r), shell
detection, and bond-orientational order twice. One of these should be
a forward reference only.

Chapter 2: Machine Learning

Strengths.

The PINN section (§2.6) is the strongest part of this chapter. The
theoretical difficulties — Sobolev approximation, spectral bias,
stability versus residual — are described with precision unusual for a
physics thesis, and the connection to the conditioning appendices is
prepared carefully.

Weaknesses.

The chapter suffers from a structural problem: it is a textbook survey
that could have been written before any of the physics was done. The
sections on SGD, momentum, Adam, universal approximation, and the
bias-variance tradeoff contain almost no original perspective and no
forward linkage to the specific choices made in Chapters 3–4. Why Mish
over GELU? Why the particular initialization used? These are mentioned
as relevant but never connected.  The Deep Sets section (§2.4)
contains a significant claim — "Deep Sets provide results of
comparable quality to Hartree-Fock, essentially missing correlation
physics completely without additional correlation terms" — that is
attributed to Haas with a placeholder citation marker. This is a
load-bearing claim that justifies the entire pairwise branch
architecture. It needs a proper citation and a brief quantitative
illustration, not a placeholder.  The activation function comparison
figure (Figure 2.1) shows derivatives up to third order. This is
well-motivated for PINN stability, but the figure caption does not
explain why the third derivative matters here. For a reader not
already familiar with the spectral bias literature, this is opaque.

Chapter 3: The Ansatz

Strengths.

This is the best chapter in the thesis. The design logic is explicit:
each architectural choice is motivated by a specific physical or
mathematical requirement, and the cost of each choice is stated. The
discussion of safe pair features — why raw distances destabilize the
Laplacian, and how soft-core features with bounded derivatives resolve
this — is precisely the kind of reasoning that distinguishes a
physics-informed neural network from a generic one. The backflow
formulation is clean and the center-of-mass neutrality condition is
handled correctly.  The three-body sensitivity ratio in Table 3.1 is a
nice result. The collapse at ω=0.001 (ratio 1.05×) connects directly
to the Wigner-molecule physics developed in Chapter 6, and the author
draws this connection explicitly.


Weaknesses.

The "cell view" section (§3.6) is tantalizing but underdeveloped. The
claim that message-passing can in principle represent three-body and
higher correlations is correct, but the theoretical argument is
gestured at rather than made. The empirical Table 3.1 partly
compensates for this, but the reader is left wondering: what exactly
is the cell encoding at ω=0.1 that a pairwise-only network cannot?
The analytic cusp formula (Eq. 3.5) uses γ↑↓ = 1/(d−1) and γ↑↑ =
1/(d+1) in general d, but the derivation earlier in §1.6.4 gives
slopes of 1 and 1/2 specifically in 2D. These are consistent (d=2
gives 1 and 1/3, not 1/2 — the parallel-spin value in 2D is 1/3 from
Eq. B.14, not 1/2). This discrepancy between 1/(d+1)|_{d=2} = 1/3 and
the claim "γ↑↑ = 1/2" that appears implicitly in some discussions
should be made explicit and reconciled.  The notation becomes dense in
§3.4–3.5. A small architectural diagram — showing information flow
from coordinates through the particle branch, pair branch, readout,
and backflow — would substantially aid comprehension. The "cell view"
subsection attempts this verbally but a figure would be more
effective.

Chapter 4: Training the Wavefunction
Strengths.

The REINFORCE derivation is clean and the "catch-22" framing —
backflow learns at the node, the local energy diverges at the node for
imperfect wavefunctions, good sampling must exclude the node — is the
clearest articulation of a genuinely difficult conceptual point. The
honest treatment of the Langevin failure (+153% at N=20) and the
importance-sampling bug is methodologically valuable.

Weaknesses.

The chapter is organized around three "layers" but the layer numbering
in the text is inconsistent. The chapter opens saying it addresses
Layer 1, Layer 3, and Layer 3 again — apparently layers do not
correspond uniquely to chapters, but the reader is not told this
clearly.  The proposal distribution (Eq. 4.3) — a mixture of three
isotropic Gaussians — is described as providing "coarse coverage."
This is true but invites scrutiny: how were the three widths chosen?
The text says they scale as 1/√ω but does not specify the ratios
between components. This matters because the ESS analysis in §4.3.2
and the entire reliability analysis in §6.4.5 depend critically on the
proposal quality. The proposal design deserves a dedicated paragraph.
The two-stage pipeline (§4.5) introduces cascade warm-starting as a
key technique for low ω, but the cascade sequence ω:
1.0→0.5→0.1→0.01→0.001 is mentioned here without any explanation of
why this particular spacing was chosen. Is it ad hoc? Is it motivated
by the crossover physics? The results chapter shows it works; this
chapter should say why it was expected to.

Chapter 5: Diagnostics

Strengths. The shell-resolved g(r) reconstruction with cosine
similarity as a validation metric is methodologically sound and
original. The Lindemann-type angular ratio λ_φ (Eq. 5.5) adapted to
finite rings is a clean contribution. The representation analysis
design (PCA on held-out blocks, entropy effective rank, PC ablations)
is rigorous and appropriately cautious about circular validation.

Weaknesses.

The chapter's relationship to §1.7 is never resolved. Both sections
define g(r), automatic shell detection, and orientational order with
slightly different notation and threshold values — τ ∈ [2.0, 3.0] in
§1.7 versus τ ∈ [2.5, 3.5] in §5.1.2. This is likely an editing
artifact, but it will confuse careful readers. One of these sections
should be the canonical definition; the other should be eliminated or
explicitly deferred.  The representation analysis (§5.2) is
technically careful but its motivation is incompletely stated. Why
should we care whether the correlator lives on a low-dimensional
manifold? The results in §6.3 provide the payoff — a compact axis that
reorganizes across regimes — but this is not anticipated here. A brief
paragraph motivating what the analysis will reveal would improve the
chapter's internal logic.

Chapter 6: Results

Strengths.

This is the most substantial chapter and largely delivers on its
ambitions. The energy table (Table 6.1) is organized clearly with
error statistics, and the physical validation through Wigner
diagnostics is genuinely original — the shell-resolved g(r)
reconstruction with cosine similarity 0.9982 for N=12 at ω=10⁻³ is a
strong result. The force-alignment diagnostic (§6.3.6) connecting
backflow displacement direction to the classical forces is an elegant
physical interpretation.  The reliability campaign (§6.4.5) with 30
runs across seeds and recipes is methodologically sophisticated for a
master's thesis. The causal chain in Eq. 6.1 — proposal mismatch → k̂
inflation → gradient noise → run-to-run variance — is a clean unifying
explanation.

Weaknesses.

Missing DMC references. For N=6 and N=12 at ω ∈ {0.001, 0.01}, Table
6.1 shows no DMC references. The thesis presents PINN+BF energies for
these points without any external benchmark. These are precisely the
most physically interesting points — deep Wigner regime — yet they are
the least validated. The author notes this in §7.2 but the results
chapter itself should flag this more prominently with a clear
statement of what "trusted" means in the absence of DMC.  The CTNN
ablation (Table 6.4) uses wrong energy values. The table shows ECTNN =
19.833, 3.704, 0.474 for ω = 1.0, 0.1, 0.001 at N=6. But Table 6.1
reports PINN+CTNN energies of 20.1585, 3.55388, 0.140832 for the same
cases. The ablation energies are dramatically lower in the first two
cases and inconsistent with the main energy table. This appears to be
either a different ansatz configuration, a differently normalized
energy, or a reporting error. It needs explanation.  The N=20 results
are underanalyzed. Table 6.9 shows N=20 errors of +1.45% to +5.68%,
which the author attributes entirely to ESS collapse. But the
representation analysis in §6.3 does not include systematic N=20
results across the same ω grid. Given that N=20 is a full closed shell
and presumably physically interesting, this asymmetry is notable.
Figure 6.1 is potentially misleading. The relative error plot shows
PINN+BF errors flattening or decreasing at small ω for N=6 and
N=12. But there are no DMC references at ω=0.001 — the curve stops at
ω=0.01. The figure visually implies validated accuracy extending
further than it does.  The three-body sensitivity in Table 3.1 is not
revisited. The thesis finds that at ω=0.001 the cell operation reduces
to pairwise behavior (ratio 1.05×), and §6.3.1 shows the cell accounts
for only 12% of the energy at this confinement. But no discussion is
offered of whether the (3,9) Wigner-molecule structure for N=12 at
ω=0.001 — the most geometrically complex result in the thesis — is
correctly captured without beyond-pairwise correlations. This is a
gap.

Cross-Cutting Issues

Citation placeholders. Several [<citation>] markers remain in the text
— in the Introduction and in §2.4. These are not minor formatting
issues; one of them supports the central claim that Deep Sets without
a pair branch matches only HF quality. These must be resolved before
submission.  Notation consistency. The backflow displacement is
variously ∆β(R̃), ∆x, δx̃, and ∆xi across chapters. A notation table
would help considerably.  The preface is exceptional. The observation
that stochastic reconfiguration, REINFORCE, and the Fisher information
matrix are the same mathematics discovered independently by
physicists, reinforcement learning researchers, and statisticians is
genuinely insightful, and the writing is unusually good. This quality
is not consistently maintained through the technical chapters.

Summary Assessment

The core scientific contributions — MCMC-free training to near-DMC
accuracy, the collocation-backflow catch-22, the topology-resolved
Wigner diagnostics, the force-alignment signature of the crossover —
are real and publishable. The thesis would be strengthened primarily
by resolving the citation placeholders, clarifying the energy
inconsistency in the CTNN ablation table, and being more explicit
about what is and is not validated at ω=0.001.~
